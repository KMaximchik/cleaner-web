Privacy Policy for Cleaner Kit AI

This Privacy Policy explains how JDG HRYSHYN ALIAKSEI, the publisher of Cleaner Kit AI ("we", "us", or "our"), processes information when you use the Cleaner Kit AI mobile application for iOS and the related website pages published for the app.

This policy reflects the current feature set of Cleaner Kit AI and should be updated whenever the app introduces new data flows, new connected services, or materially changes how the existing features work.

1. Summary

  • Cleaner Kit AI mainly processes photos, videos, contacts, and private files you choose to organize in the app.
  • We do not sell your personal data and we do not use cross-app tracking in the current version of Cleaner Kit AI.
  • The app currently uses Firebase Analytics and Firebase Crashlytics to measure usage and app stability.
  • Secret Library content is stored locally on your device; contact backups may be stored in your iCloud account or locally if iCloud is unavailable.
  • If you deny a permission, the related feature may not work until you enable access again in iOS Settings.

Photos and videos

If you grant photo library access, Cleaner Kit AI can inspect your media library to identify duplicate photos, similar photos, screenshots, blurred items, and videos. Based on the current codebase, this analysis is performed locally on-device.

Contacts

If you grant contact access, Cleaner Kit AI can read your contacts to identify duplicates, show incomplete contact entries, merge contacts, create backups, restore backups, and import selected contacts into Secret Library.

Secret Library

Secret Library allows you to import photos, videos, documents, and contacts into a private area inside the app. The current implementation stores this content in the app's local private storage. If you enable Face ID, the app uses iOS biometric authentication to help protect access to Secret Library.

Analytics and crash data

Cleaner Kit AI currently integrates Firebase Analytics and Firebase Crashlytics. Based on the current implementation, analytics is collected from app launch and includes low-cardinality usage data such as screens opened, feature actions, permission statuses, counters, and sizes. Crashlytics receives crash and diagnostic information needed to maintain the app.

Based on the current analytics specification and the reviewed code, the app is designed not to send file names, local file URLs, contact names, contact phone numbers, contact email addresses, pin codes, or other direct content data to analytics.

3. Sources of Personal Data

We obtain personal data from the following sources:

  • directly from your device and your actions in the app, such as when you grant permissions or use a feature;
  • from iOS system frameworks, such as Photos, Contacts, Camera, Microphone, Face ID, Keychain, and iCloud, when you choose to use those features;
  • from Firebase services, which receive analytics and crash-related data from the app;
  • from imported files, contacts, photos, or videos that you select for processing or storage in the app.

4. How We Use Data

We use information for the following purposes:

  • To provide the core Cleaner Kit AI functionality you request.
  • To scan and organize your media and contacts on your device.
  • To create contact backups and restore them when you choose to do so.
  • To protect Secret Library with passcode and biometric access controls.
  • To improve usability, measure feature adoption, and fix crashes.
  • To maintain app security, detect malfunctions, and troubleshoot issues.

Legal bases

Where applicable under privacy laws such as the GDPR, we generally rely on the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract: to provide requested app features such as cleaning, backups, and Secret Library.
  • Consent: for access to photos, contacts, camera, microphone, and biometrics, where the operating system requires your permission.
  • Legitimate interests: to understand app usage, improve reliability, and prevent abuse, provided those interests are not overridden by your rights.
  • Legal obligations: where retention or disclosure is required by applicable law.

5. Sharing of Information

We do not sell your personal data. We do not use App Tracking Transparency for cross-app tracking in the current version of Cleaner Kit AI, and the current app privacy manifest states that tracking is disabled.

We may share information with the following categories of service providers:

  • Google Firebase Analytics, to receive app analytics data.
  • Google Firebase Crashlytics, to receive crash and diagnostics data.
  • Apple iCloud, if you use contact backup features that sync or store backups through your iCloud account.

We may also disclose information if required by law, legal process, or to protect our rights, users, or the security of the app.

Third-party links

The app and related website may include links to privacy, terms, support, or store pages. Third-party services have their own privacy practices, and you should review their policies separately.

6. Retention

  • Photos, videos, contacts, documents, and Secret Library items remain on your device or in your iCloud account until you delete them or remove the app, subject to iOS behavior.
  • Contact backups remain available until you delete them from the app or your iCloud storage.
  • App settings remain stored until you reset them, remove the app, or clear the relevant local data.
  • Analytics and crash data are retained for as long as reasonably necessary for product analytics and stability, subject to Google Firebase retention settings and applicable law.

7. Your Privacy Choices and Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights such as:

  • accessing the personal data we hold about you;
  • requesting correction or deletion;
  • requesting restriction of processing;
  • objecting to certain processing;
  • requesting data portability; and
  • withdrawing consent for permissions you granted on your device.

Because much of Cleaner Kit AI's functionality is local-first, you can often exercise control directly by deleting content from the app, revoking iOS permissions, deleting iCloud backups, or uninstalling the app.

You may also have the right to lodge a complaint with the data protection authority in your place of residence or, where applicable, with the Polish supervisory authority, the President of the Personal Data Protection Office (UODO).

How to manage permissions

  • Photos: iOS Settings > Privacy & Security > Photos.
  • Contacts: iOS Settings > Privacy & Security > Contacts.
  • Camera: iOS Settings > Privacy & Security > Camera.
  • Microphone: iOS Settings > Privacy & Security > Microphone.
  • Face ID: iOS Settings > Face ID & Passcode or the app-specific settings screen.

California notice

If you are a California resident, you may have rights under the CCPA/CPRA, including the right to know, access, delete, and correct certain personal information, and the right not to be discriminated against for exercising your privacy rights. Cleaner Kit AI does not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising in the current version.

8. International Transfers

Service providers such as Google may process information on servers located outside your country. Where required, we rely on appropriate safeguards under applicable law for such international transfers, including where relevant the European Commission's adequacy decisions or Standard Contractual Clauses and related supplementary measures made available by our service providers.

9. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures intended to protect information. The app uses iOS security mechanisms, app sandboxing, local private storage, and Keychain storage for sensitive secrets such as tokens where applicable. However, no method of electronic storage or transmission is completely secure.

10. Children's Privacy

Cleaner Kit AI is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information to us, please contact us so we can review the issue.
